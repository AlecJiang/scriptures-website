Scripture 1 – John 3:16

John 3:16

“For God so loved the world, that he gave his only begotten Son, that whosoever believeth in him should not perish, but have everlasting life.”

What This Scripture Teaches about Christ

This verse is one of the most famous descriptions of Heavenly Father's love and the mission of Jesus Christ. It teaches that the reason Christ came into the world was because of love—love for the whole world and for each individual person. The gift of the “only begotten Son” shows that the Atonement was not an accident or a backup plan. It was a loving, deliberate choice made for our salvation.

The scripture also emphasizes belief. Eternal life is promised to “whosoever believeth in him.” True belief in Christ is not just agreeing that He exists; it means trusting Him, relying on His grace, and trying to follow Him day by day. Because of Christ, we do not have to be stuck in our sins or our past mistakes. Through Him, we are offered forgiveness and a new life with God.

How I Can Apply This Verse

When I feel discouraged or unworthy, this scripture reminds me that God already showed how much He loves me by sending His Son. I do not have to earn His love; it is already given. What I can do is respond to that love by believing in Christ, repenting, and trying again. Remembering this verse helps me be more patient with myself and more forgiving of others, because God loved “the world,” not just a few select people.

In my daily life, I can apply this scripture by choosing to trust Christ in specific situations—like school stress, family problems, or personal weaknesses. Instead of thinking only about my own strength, I can look to Him and remember that His purpose is to give me everlasting life, not to let me fail.
