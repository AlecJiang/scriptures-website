Scripture 2 – John 14:6

John 14:6

“Jesus saith unto him, I am the way, the truth, and the life: no man cometh unto the Father, but by me.”

What This Scripture Teaches about Christ

In this verse, the Savior does not just point to a way or give directions; He says that He Himself is the way. The path back to the Father is not a list of rules that we follow on our own. It is a relationship with a living, loving Redeemer. Christ is also “the truth,” meaning that in a world full of confusion and changing opinions, He is completely honest, steady, and trustworthy.

Calling Himself “the life” reminds me that every good and lasting thing is connected to Him. Through His Resurrection, He conquered physical and spiritual death. Because of Him, our lives can have real purpose and hope, even when we do not understand everything that is happening.

How I Can Apply This Verse

When I make decisions—about school, friends, or my future—I can ask, “Does this choice bring me closer to Christ, the way?” Instead of only asking what is easiest or most popular, I can ask what helps me follow Him better. I can also study His words more carefully, because if He is the truth, then His teachings will help me see myself and others more clearly.

Practically, this means praying in His name, reading the scriptures with real intent, going to church with a desire to worship, and trying to love people the way He did. Each small step of following Him is a step on the path that leads back to the Father.
