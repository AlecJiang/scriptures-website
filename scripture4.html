Scripture 4 – Isaiah 53:4–5

Isaiah 53:4–5

“Surely he hath borne our griefs, and carried our sorrows: yet we did esteem him stricken, smitten of God, and afflicted. But he was wounded for our transgressions, he was bruised for our iniquities: the chastisement of our peace was upon him; and with his stripes we are healed.”

What This Scripture Teaches about Christ

Isaiah saw, many years before Christ was born, what the Savior would suffer for us. These verses describe the Atonement in very personal language: Christ bore our griefs and carried our sorrows. He was wounded and bruised not for His own sins, but for ours. The phrase “with his stripes we are healed” suggests that the very wounds He received are the source of our spiritual and emotional healing.

This prophecy shows that Christ is not only aware of our sins but also of our pains, disappointments, and heartaches. He understands what it feels like to be rejected, misunderstood, and hurt. Because He has already carried these things, He can comfort us perfectly and offer us peace that we cannot find anywhere else.

How I Can Apply This Verse

When I feel guilty, ashamed, or broken, I can remember that Christ has already paid the price so that I can repent and be forgiven. I do not have to stay stuck in my past. I can choose to turn to Him, confess my sins, and accept His mercy. “The chastisement of our peace was upon him” means that He suffered so that I could have peace instead of constant fear.

I can also apply this verse by trusting that Christ understands my pain. When I am sad or lonely, I can picture Him carrying those sorrows and walking beside me. Knowing that He has felt what I feel helps me not to give up and to keep moving forward with faith.
