Scripture 3 – Matthew 11:28–30

Matthew 11:28–30

“Come unto me, all ye that labour and are heavy laden, and I will give you rest. Take my yoke upon you, and learn of me; for I am meek and lowly in heart: and ye shall find rest unto your souls. For my yoke is easy, and my burden is light.”

What This Scripture Teaches about Christ

These verses show Christ as a gentle and compassionate teacher. He sees the people who are “heavy laden” and invites them to come to Him, not to a program or a system, but to a person who understands their struggles. The image of a “yoke” suggests working side by side with Him. He does not remove all burdens instantly, but He carries them with us so that they become light enough to bear.

Christ also describes Himself as “meek and lowly in heart.” Even though He is the Son of God, He is humble and approachable. This teaches me that God is not distant or harsh. Through Christ, the Father reaches out to us in kindness and invites us to find rest “unto [our] souls,” a deep peace that goes beyond just feeling relaxed for a moment.

How I Can Apply This Verse

When I feel overwhelmed by homework, responsibilities, expectations, or my own mistakes, I can remember that Christ specifically invites people like that to come to Him. I can come to Him through honest prayer, by telling Him exactly how I feel, and by asking for strength and guidance.

Taking His yoke upon me also means learning of Him—studying His life, copying His patience, and trying to serve others the way He did. When I focus on Him instead of only on my problems, I often notice that the weight on my mind and heart becomes lighter, even if the situation itself has not changed yet.
