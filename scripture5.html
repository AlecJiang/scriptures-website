Scripture 5 – Philippians 4:13

Philippians 4:13

“I can do all things through Christ which strengtheneth me.”

What This Scripture Teaches about Christ

Paul wrote these words while he was facing difficult circumstances, yet he still expressed complete confidence in Christ. This verse does not mean that we will suddenly be able to do anything we want; instead, it means that whatever the Lord asks us to do, He will give us the strength to accomplish it. Christ is the source of real spiritual power.

The focus of the verse is not on “I can do all things” but on “through Christ.” On our own we are limited, but with His help we can endure trials, keep the commandments, and serve others in ways that would be impossible by ourselves. Christ strengthens us from the inside out—changing our desires, our thoughts, and our courage.

How I Can Apply This Verse

When I face something that feels too hard for me—like speaking in front of a group, moving to a new place, learning a difficult subject, or resisting temptation—I can remember this promise. Instead of telling myself “I can’t,” I can pray and say, “Through Christ, I can try again.” This verse helps me replace fear with faith.

I can also apply it by giving Christ credit for my successes. When things go well, I can remember that the strength came from Him and thank Him in my prayers. Recognizing His hand in my life increases my gratitude and keeps me humble.
